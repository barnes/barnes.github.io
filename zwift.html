I've recently gotten myself hooked on Zwift, a virtual reality (or augmented reality?) indoor bike training system that allows for virtual bike racing in real time. Its an awesome amount of fun, and an awesome workout. The best part is, the data needed to do virtual races also means the nerdy cyclists like myself can explore heaps of data and try to identify trends, monitor issues, etc. From heart rate sensors, to cadence and power meters, every session fills up with data. On this page we'll look at some of that data. Perhaps even pull data live from the Zwift API.
